\abstract{
The recent adoption of Deep Neural Networks (DNNs) in safety-critical applications has increased the need to verify their correctness formally. At first glance, DNN verifiers solve this issue. Unfortunately, ensuring the correctness of DNN verifiers by fully formally proving the correctness of their implementation is a very complex task. To tackle this matter, DNN verifiers have been provided with the ability to produce proof certificates of the proofs they generate. The state-of-the-art DNN verifier, Marabou, includes a proof checker implemented in C++, which suffers from numerical instability; this inevitably raises doubts about its reliability and, by extension, its soundness. Recent progress in this area has led to the implementation of a fully correct proof checker in Imandra, a programming language and an interactive theorem prover (ITP). However, we are still lacking an implementation of a proof checker for Marabou in a more widely adopted ITP. Therefore, in this paper, an implementation based on the development carried out in Imandra is proposed in Rocq. The results aim to bring the ecosystem of DNN verifiers into the spotlight of the theorem proving community by adopting a proof checker for Marabou in Rocq, a well-established ITP.
}
